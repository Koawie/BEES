Responsibilities

The main duties of worker bees in the colony are; hive cleaning, royal jelly and wax secretion, honeycomb knitting, brood care, ventilation of the hive, care and feeding of the queen, guarding the hive, carrying nectar, pollen, propolis, water to the hive and ensuring the maturation of honey.

Formation

Worker bees come from fertilized eggs. Depending on the strength of the colony and the season, the number of worker bees in the colony varies between 10,000-20,000 during the winter months, while their numbers increase gradually in the spring and can be 60,000-80,000 in the summer months. The strength of colonies is determined by the presence of worker bees they have.

Death

They live for 4-9 months in winter and 6 weeks in summer. Worker bees have a barbed sting, and when they sting a living thing with this sting, they drop their sting and die.

Worker Bees

Worker bees are female bees that do not have the capacity to mate in a bee colony. Although all worker bees are female, only the queen bee has the capacity to mate among the females in a bee colony. Apart from these, there are few drones in the colony whose job is to inseminate the queen. Male bees die during mating.
