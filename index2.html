Birth

When the queen queen leaves the main colony with the bee swarm to establish a new colony, the first queen candidate to hatch tries to destroy the other queen candidates. If two queen candidates hatch at the same time, they will fight to the death.

Reproductive

The queen often mates in midair with multiple drones to maintain genetic diversity in the colony. These mating flights may be repeated several days in a row. Then the queen begins to lay eggs and does not leave the hive except for flights to establish a new colony.

Death

Queen bees can live up to 5 years. When the queen accidentally dies or her egg laying slows down, the worker bees start to raise a new queen candidate. The new queen ignores the queen mother and the queen mother disappears after a while.

Queen Bees

A queen bee is typically an adult, mated female (gyne) that lives in a colony or hive of honey bees. With fully developed reproductive organs, the queen is usually the mother of most, if not all, of the bees in the beehive.[

Queens are developed from larvae selected by worker bees and specially fed in order to become sexually mature. There is normally only one adult, mated queen in a hive, in which case the bees will usually follow and fiercely protect her.
